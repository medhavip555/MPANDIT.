How Crime Shapes Indego Bike Usage

Drexel University Capstone · Python · R · GeoPandas · 2025
Spatial RegressionPythonGeoPandasR StudioOpenDataPhillyPolicy
Read the full paper

Research question: Does proximity to crime reduce Indego bike-share ridership in Philadelphia?

What we built

Our team analyzed 1M+ trips across 250 stations against Philadelphia crime data from OpenDataPhilly. We built 200m buffer zones around each station, classified crimes into severe, medium-severe, and non-severe categories, and ran both uncontrolled and controlled linear regression models. Control variables included neighborhood population density and census-tract poverty rates sourced via Python + Census API.

The counterintuitive finding

We expected crime proximity to reduce ridership. It didn't. Every quarter showed a positive correlation between crime count and trip activity — stations in high-crime areas were busier, not quieter. Our log-log regression (β=0.37, p<0.001) confirmed significance but low explanatory power (R²=7.4%), pointing to confounders like transit access and business density.

After adding controls

Introducing poverty rate and population density raised our R² to 20.6%. A 1% increase in crime was associated with a 0.35% increase in trips. Higher poverty correlated with fewer trips, consistent with economic access barriers. High-crime stations showed greater variance in ridership — likely driven by proximity to transit hubs and commercial areas.

Policy implication

Indego can place stations in high-crime, high-demand areas without fear of ridership drop. The real lever is transit connectivity and economic accessibility, not crime avoidance. Future work should examine business density and commute patterns as primary predictors.

Khoj (खोज — meaning "search" or "discovery" in Nepali) is an AI-powered mountaineering assistant designed for trekkers and expedition teams navigating Nepal's high-altitude terrain.

My role

I served as Product Owner and data lead — scoping user requirements from trekker personas, defining the product roadmap, and leading the geospatial analysis component. The team built in 48 hours under a compressed hackathon timeline.

What Khoj does

  • Geospatial route optimization using GeoPandas and elevation data
  • NLP-powered Q&A for trail conditions, altitude acclimatization, and safety
  • Real-time hazard awareness — avalanche zones, glacier crossings

Nepal receives 300,000+ trekkers annually. Many lack access to up-to-date safety data in remote zones with limited connectivity. Khoj bridges that gap — a digital sherpa built from local knowledge and satellite data.

Airline Merger Causal Analysis

Senior Thesis · Drexel University · Stata · Difference-in-Differences · 2025–2026
StataDiff-in-DiffPanel DataDB1B DataT-100BTSCausal Inference

Research question: Did the 2013 American Airlines–US Airways merger raise fares and reduce service quality on overlap routes?

Data & method

Applied difference-in-differences (DiD) methodology using three federal datasets: DB1B (fare data), T-100 (traffic and capacity), and BTS on-time performance. Overlap routes between the two carriers served as the treatment group; non-overlap routes served as controls.

Key findings

  • Modest but statistically significant fare increases on overlap routes post-merger
  • Service quality (on-time performance) was maintained or slightly improved post-merger — counter to the anticompetitive narrative
  • Placebo test failed, indicating potential parallel trends violations — a key limitation acknowledged in the analysis

Methodological nuance

The failed placebo test suggests pre-merger trends may not have been fully parallel between treatment and control groups, which limits causal interpretation. This finding drove further investigation into route-level heterogeneity and robustness checks using alternative control group definitions.

Skills demonstrated

Panel data fixed effects, IV/2SLS, regression discontinuity, event studies — full causal inference toolkit applied to a real merger with documented DOJ oversight.
